Local Development

How can I test my application locally?

In general, if you want to test your Slack application, you will be required to provide URLs that host on a server for Slack to call your endpoints. This is not a problem if you have a full server ready.

However, it would be great if development could be simplified even more by allowing you to test your requests from your machine without constantly redeploying your work to a hosting webserver.

In this section, we will introduce a tool ngrok that can help you to achieve it.

ngrok is a third party service, we are not going to provide many details for it. Please feel free to learn it more from its official website. Also, there are many other free alternatives on internet.

Setting up ngrok

Step 1: Download ngrok

Go to https://ngrok.com/ and download the version that corresponds to your platform.

Step 2: Install ngrok

Go to your ngrok directory, and run:

./ngrok http 4390

or if you want ngrok to listen your local custom host url:

./ngrok http -host-header=rewrite slack.test:80

slack.test:80 is a localhost custom name. You need to change it to your own one.
